    *   **Bottom Slings (Spreader Bar to Girder):** Two (2) wire rope or synthetic slings in a choker hitch. Each sling must have a minimum rated capacity of **6,000 lbs** in a choker hitch configuration (chokers are typically de-rated).
    *   **Shackles:** Four (4) screw-pin anchor shackles. Each shackle must have a Working Load Limit (WLL) of at least **3.25 tons (6,500 lbs)**. Ensure shackles are sized correctly for the slings.
    *   **Edge Protection:** If synthetic slings are used, engineered edge protection ("cut-proof" pads) is mandatory where slings contact the steel edges.
    *   **Taglines:** A minimum of two (2) non-conductive taglines will be used on all long members to control the load.

*   **General Rigging Requirements:**
    *   **Inspection:** All rigging hardware (slings, shackles, hooks, etc.) will be visually inspected by a qualified rigger before each shift and before each use. Damaged or defective gear will be immediately removed from service.
    *   **WLL:** All rigging components must have a legible manufacturer's tag indicating the Working Load Limit.
    *   **Load Control:** The load will not be allowed to travel over personnel. Hands and fingers will be kept clear of pinch points during landing and connection.

### **3. FALL PROTECTION PLAN (OSHA 1926 SUBPART R)**

Fall protection is mandatory and will be enforced at 100%.

*   **Trigger Heights for Fall Protection:**
    *   **Connectors:** When working at heights over two stories or **30 feet**, whichever is less.
    *   **All Other Ironworkers (Deckers, Welders, Bolters):** When working at heights over **15 feet**.
*   **Primary Fall Protection Method: Personal Fall Arrest System (PFAS)**
    *   **Components:**
        1.  **Anchorage:** Must be capable of supporting 5,000 lbs per person attached. Approved anchor points include primary structural members (beams, columns) or engineered horizontal lifelines. Beam clamps and retractable lanyards are preferred.
        2.  **Harness:** Full-body harness, inspected daily by the user.
        3.  **Connector:** Lanyards (shock-absorbing) or Self-Retracting Lifelines (SRLs). A 100% tie-off policy is in effect (using a dual-leg lanyard or two separate connectors) when transitioning between anchor points.
*   **Controlled Decking Zone (CDZ):**
    *   A CDZ may be established for leading-edge decking work where other fall protection methods are infeasible.
    *   The CDZ is limited in access to only those workers engaged in leading-edge work.
    *   The CDZ will be clearly marked with control lines and will not be more than 90 feet wide and 90 feet deep from any leading edge.
    *   Workers in a CDZ must be trained on the specific procedures.
*   **Fall Rescue Plan:**
    *   A plan and equipment for prompt rescue of a fallen worker will be available on-site. The primary method will be an **aerial lift** positioned to access all work areas.
    *   All personnel will be trained on the rescue plan and the hazards of suspension trauma. The goal is to rescue a suspended worker in **under 5 minutes**.

### **4. ERECTION STABILITY & BRACING PLAN**

The structure must remain stable throughout the entire erection process.

*   **Erection Sequence:** The erection will follow the sequence detailed on the approved Erection Drawings provided by the steel fabricator/engineer of record.
*   **Column Anchorage (OSHA 1926.755(a)):**
    *   All columns must be anchored by a minimum of **four (4) anchor rods**.
    *   Columns will not be released from the crane until they are secured with a sufficient number of anchor rod nuts tightened to a snug-tight condition.
*   **Temporary Bracing:**
    *   Temporary bracing (wire rope guys, temporary struts) will be installed in each bay before any plumbing or further erection in that bay continues.
    *   The structure will be plumbed, aligned, and braced as erection progresses.
    *   Per OSHA, there shall not be more than eight stories or 48 feet of unfinished bolting or welding above the foundation or the uppermost permanently secured floor. For this 3-storey structure, this means each floor must be made stable before proceeding to the next.
*   **Permanent Bracing & Connections:**
    *   Permanent bracing, moment-resisting connections, and diaphragm decking will be installed as early as possible in the erection sequence to provide permanent stability to the frame.
    *   Metal decking will be laid and fastened (welded/shot) promptly after the steel frame is in place to create a working surface and act as a structural diaphragm.
