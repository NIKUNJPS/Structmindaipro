### **1.0 Hard Clashes (Member-to-Member Interference)**

A hard clash represents a direct physical interference between two or more solid model elements. These are critical issues that must be resolved prior to fabrication.

*   **Finding:** A vertical brace gusset plate at the column-beam intersection on Level 3 directly interferes with the top flange of an infill beam. The gusset plate geometry was not clipped to accommodate the incoming beam.
    *   **Location:** Level 3 @ Grid D-5
    *   **Severity:** **Critical**



### **2.0 Soft Clashes (Clearance Violations)**

A soft clash occurs when the clearance between elements violates a predefined rule, such as required space for installation, fireproofing, or maintenance. For this analysis, a minimum erection/fireproofing clearance of 1/2" (12.7mm) was assumed.

*   **Finding:** Two parallel infill beams have been modeled with a 1/4" gap between their flanges. This is insufficient for proper spray-applied fireproofing and violates standard erection tolerance.
    *   **Location:** Level 2, between Grids F-3 and F-4
    *   **Severity:** **Medium**

### **3.0 Cross-Trade Clashes (Structural vs. MEP)**

These clashes involve interference between the structural frame and other disciplines, such as Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing. The analysis focused on the designated Level 2 Mechanical Room.

*   **Finding 1:** A large rectangular HVAC supply duct (48"x24") runs directly through the web of a primary W30x108 girder. No web penetration has been designed or detailed.
    *   **Location:** Level 2 Mechanical Room @ Grid B-3
    *   **Severity:** **High**

*   **Finding 2:** A 6" Fire Protection main pipe is modeled with its top-of-pipe elevation conflicting with the bottom flange of multiple W18x50 floor beams. The pipe run has zero clearance.
    *   **Location:** Level 2 Mechanical Room, running along Grid Line C between C-2 and C-5
    *   **Severity:** **High**



### **4.0 Connection & Tool Clearance Violations**

This check ensures adequate space is available for bolting, welding, and tool access during erection. The focus was on moment end-plate connections as requested.

*   **Finding:** A W24x94 beam frames into the web of a W14x120 column with a moment end-plate connection. The proximity of the column flange provides insufficient clearance for an impact wrench to access and tighten the bolts on the flange side of the connection.
    *   **Location:** Level 2 @ Grid A-4
    *   **Severity:** **High**
