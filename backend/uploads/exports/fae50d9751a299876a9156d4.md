*   **Spreader Bar:** A properly rated and certified spreader bar of sufficient length to ensure slings remain near-vertical (80-90 degrees) shall be used.
*   **Slings (Top):** Two (2) wire rope or chain slings connecting the crane hook to the spreader bar. Each sling must have a vertical Working Load Limit (WLL) of at least 5 tons.
*   **Slings (Bottom):** Two (2) wire rope choker slings or synthetic web slings (with edge protection) connecting the spreader bar to the girder. Each sling must have a vertical WLL of at least 5 tons. Slings will be basket-hitched around the girder at engineered pick points.
*   **Shackles:** All shackles must be alloy steel, properly sized for the slings, with a WLL greater than the anticipated load. Screw-pin shackles must have the pin fully seated and secured.

**2.2. General Rigging Practices:**
*   **Qualified Rigger:** All rigging must be selected and attached by a qualified rigger.
*   **Taglines:** At least two (2) non-conductive taglines will be used on all column and girder lifts to control load movement.
*   **Sling Protection:** Edge protection ("softeners") must be used whenever slings are in contact with sharp edges of steel.
*   **Center of Gravity:** Rigging shall be attached above the load's center of gravity to ensure stability.

### **3. Fall Protection Plan (OSHA 1926 Subpart R)**

Fall protection is mandatory for all personnel working at heights.

*   **General Requirement:** All employees on a walking/working surface with an unprotected side or edge more than 15 feet above a lower level shall be protected by guardrail systems, safety net systems, or a Personal Fall Arrest System (PFAS).
*   **Connectors (15 to 30 feet):** While working as a connector, employees between 15 and 30 feet must be provided with and wear a complete PFAS. They must be tied off when not actively receiving or connecting members.
*   **All Personnel (Above 30 feet):** All personnel, including connectors, must be tied off using a PFAS 100% of the time.
*   **Personal Fall Arrest System (PFAS):**
    *   **A - Anchorage:** Anchor points must be independent of any platform support and capable of supporting 5,000 lbs. per person attached. Beam clamps, beam straps, or other engineered anchor points will be utilized.
    *   **B - Body Harness:** Full-body harnesses will be worn by all personnel working at height.
    *   **C - Connecting Device:** Lanyards (shock-absorbing) or Self-Retracting Lifelines (SRLs) will be used. Lanyards will be selected to ensure fall distance does not exceed 6 feet.
*   **Controlled Decking Zone (CDZ):**
    *   A CDZ may be established during metal decking installation over 15 feet.
    *   Access will be limited to trained and authorized deckers.
    *   The CDZ boundary will be clearly marked.
    *   Safety deckers in a CDZ are not required to be tied off until they are no longer engaged in leading-edge work.
*   **Rescue Plan:** In the event of a fall, a prompt rescue plan is in place. This includes having a lift (e.g., aerial lift) on standby, trained personnel, and emergency contact information readily available. Suspension trauma will be addressed in training.

### **4. Erection Stability & Bracing Plan**

The structure must be stabilized at all times during erection.

*   **Anchor Bolts:** All columns must be erected on a foundation with a minimum of four (4) anchor bolts. No column shall be released from the crane until it is secured with at least four bolts.
*   **Initial Connections:** Before releasing the crane load, beams and girders must be secured with a minimum of two (2) bolts per connection, installed in accordance with OSHA 1926.756(c)(1).
*   **Temporary Bracing:** The structure will be stabilized with temporary guys, cables, or structural members as detailed in the erection drawings. No more than eight stories of steel shall be erected before the structure is fully plumbed and bolted. For this 3-storey structure, the frame must be plumbed and stabilized before proceeding to the next tier.
*   **Diagonal Bracing:** Permanent diagonal bracing will be installed as soon as practical. These members are critical for structural integrity.
*   **Load Placement:** No construction loads (e.g., bundles of decking, equipment) are allowed on the unbraced steel frame. Decking may only be placed on a fully bolted and braced bay.
