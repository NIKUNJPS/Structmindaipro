### 6. HIDDEN-COST EXPOSURE

The following items represent potential sources of significant cost escalation if not explicitly defined and controlled in the contract documents. An RFI should be issued to clarify any ambiguity.

*   **Bolt Grade Creep:** The estimate assumes A325 bolts. A contractual change to A490 bolts would increase bolt material cost by over 60% (from ~$0.70/set to ~$1.15/set) and require different pre-installation verification procedures per RCSC §7.
*   **Third-Party NDT Scope:** The bid includes standard in-house QA/QC and visual weld inspection (VT). If ultrasonic (UT) or magnetic particle (MT) testing is required on CJP welds or other specific connections, this will add direct third-party inspection costs and potentially impact shop schedule.
*   **Coatings Specification:** The specified SP6 with shop primer is standard. A change to a high-performance, multi-coat system (e.g., SSPC-SP10 with zinc/epoxy/urethane) would increase coating costs by a factor of 8-10x and require a climate-controlled paint bay.
*   **Site Access & Erection Constraints:** The erection price is predicated on unimpeded site access for cranes and material delivery. Any restrictions (e.g., tight urban infill, power lines, work-hour limits) will require more expensive crane solutions and increase erection time, directly impacting cost.
